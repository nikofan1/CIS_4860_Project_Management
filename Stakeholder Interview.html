Stakeholder Interviews

Interview 1: Anthony Choi

Name: Anthony Choi Date of Meeting: 12/02/2025

Selection Rationale

Anthony is one of my high school friends and currently works as a Software Engineer at Boeing. He specializes in Java, SQL, and Azure. He holds a B.S. from UC Merced and also has experience in freelance web development.

Conversation Summary

I shared with Anthony that I am working toward becoming a web developer and that I am currently learning HTML, SQL, and JavaScript. I also showed him my project charter and explained how I am breaking down the project step by step. He appreciated my approach and said it reflected how real projects are managed in software engineering.

Since he works on many projects, I asked him, “What qualities do you look for in people you work with?” He explained that he values reliability, clear communication, and strong collaboration. He also emphasized the importance of showing respect for others and staying flexible when circumstances change.

Key Insights

  • Clear and respectful communication strengthens collaboration and helps prevent misunderstandings.
  • Flexibility is essential in project management, as it allows teams to adapt and continue moving forward when situations shift.

Interview 2: Frederick Rena

Name: Frederick Rena Date of Meeting: 12/04/2025

Selection Rationale

Frederick was recommended to me by Anthony as another professional who could support my project. He is an Information Technology Support Engineer at American Airlines, specializing in software support, hardware troubleshooting, and network diagnostics.

Conversation Summary

Before beginning our conversation, I asked if I could record the discussion for my project, and he agreed. I shared my project charter with him and explained that I am taking a project management course. I then asked him, “How do you use project management in your job?”

Frederick explained that project management helps him stay organized and ensure that tasks are completed efficiently and on schedule. In IT operations, he breaks projects into smaller, manageable steps to track progress and identify issues early.

Key Insights

  • Breaking complex tasks into smaller steps improves efficiency and reduces the likelihood of errors.
  • Clear communication and coordination with others contribute to more successful outcomes.
