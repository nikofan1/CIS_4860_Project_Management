SDLC Lessons Learned

Date SDLC Stage What was the problem? What was the consequence? Lesson Learned Advice
09/05/25 Initiation Project goals were broad and not defined at the start. Extra time was needed to refine the scope and clarify deliverables. Always create clear and measurable goals during initiation to avoid rework.
09/18/25 Planning Underestimated the time required to complete LinkedIn Learning courses and QuickStart readings. Schedule delays and increased workload later in the semester. Build buffer time into your plan and realistically estimate effort for learning-based tasks.
10/02/25 Execution Portfolio feedback required multiple revisions; initial version lacked clarity and organization. Added iterations delayed launch of the final portfolio page. Seek early feedback and use a checklist aligned with project requirements to reduce late-stage revisions.
10/15/25 Monitoring Struggled to track progress across many tasks (certifications, portfolio, job applications). Missed a few intermediate deadlines and had to rush job applications. Use consistent tracking tools (project board, calendar reminders) to stay on schedule and visualize progress.
11/03/25 Execution Some job postings required skills not included in personal skillset (frameworks beyond HTML/CSS/JS). Extra time had to be spent researching and identifying more beginner-friendly positions. Analyze job market requirements early so you can adjust skill development plans before applying.
11/25/25 Closing Documentation of project outcomes (portfolio, applications, skills learned) took longer than expected. Final submission felt rushed. Begin closing documentation early and update it throughout the project.

Key Takeaway for Future Projects

Completing LinkedIn Learning courses took longer than planned. Future projects should build in learning curves and plan ahead for unfamiliar tools.

At times, decisions made earlier in the project were difficult to recall during final reporting. Keeping decision logs or brief notes ensures clarity on rationale and supports accountability.
